# Air Quality Monitoring Population Inequity Analysis

## Overview

- **Total Population** (2011 Census): 1,258,864,257 (1.26 billion)
- **Total Monitoring Stations**: 537
- **National Average**: 2,344,254 people per station (2.34 million per station)
- **National Average Coverage**: 0.43 stations per million people

## Key Findings

- The national average is **2.34 million people per monitoring station**
- Coverage varies widely: from **2.84** to **0.08** stations per million people
- Large states like Uttar Pradesh, Bihar, and West Bengal are significantly underserved despite high populations
- Smaller states/UTs like Delhi, Chandigarh have much better per-capita coverage
